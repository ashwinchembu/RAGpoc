SIZE GUIDE

How to Measure

Body Measurements

Bust/Chest
- Measure around the fullest part
- Keep tape level across back
- Wear non-padded bra for accuracy
- Don't pull tape too tight
- Round to nearest half inch

Waist
- Measure at natural waistline
- Usually narrowest part of torso
- Don't suck in stomach
- Keep one finger between tape and body
- Stand naturally

Hips
- Measure around fullest part
- Usually 7-9 inches below waist
- Keep tape parallel to floor
- Include buttocks in measurement
- Stand with feet together

Inseam
- Measure from crotch to ankle
- Use pants that fit well
- Measure along inner leg seam
- Best done with help
- Bare feet for accuracy

Measuring Feet:
1. Stand on paper
2. Trace foot outline
3. Measure from heel to longest toe
4. Add 0.5 inches
5. Compare to chart

Fit Tips

Between Sizes
If measurements fall between sizes:
- For fitted styles: Size up
- For loose styles: Size down
- Check specific product notes
- Read customer reviews
- When in doubt, size up

Size Variations
- Different brands may fit differently
- Check individual product size charts
- International sizing varies
- Vintage items may run smaller
- Athletic fit vs. regular fit differences

Body Type Considerations
- Pear shape: Focus on hip measurement
- Apple shape: Focus on waist/bust
- Athletic build: May need larger size for shoulders
- Petite: Check length measurements
- Tall: Look for extended sizes

Specific Item Sizing

Dresses
- Measure over undergarments
- Consider length for your height
- Check for adjustable features
- Note if dress has stretch
- Petite/Tall options available

Pants
- Waist measurement most important
- Inseam determines length
- Rise (low, mid, high) affects fit
- Consider stretch vs. rigid
- Hemming service available

Tops
- Bust/chest measurement primary
- Check shoulder width
- Note sleeve length
- Regular vs. fitted cut
- Length varies by style

Jackets/Coats
- Measure over typical clothing
- Check shoulder seam placement
- Ensure arm movement
- Consider layering space
- Length measurement important

Activewear
- Usually true to size
- Compression fit runs smaller
- Check fabric stretch
- Consider intended use
- Moisture-wicking considerations

Fit Types

Regular Fit
- Standard cut
- Comfortable room
- Not tight or loose
- Most versatile
- Best for: Most body types

Slim Fit
- Closer to body
- Tailored appearance
- Less room
- Modern style
- Best for: Athletic/slim builds

Relaxed Fit
- Generous cut
- Extra room
- Very comfortable
- Casual style
- Best for: Comfort preference

Athletic Fit
- Room in shoulders/chest
- Tapered waist
- Accommodates muscles
- Modern cut
- Best for: Athletic builds

Specialty Items

Bras
Band Size:
- Measure under bust
- Add 4-5 inches
- Round to nearest even number

Cup Size:
- Measure around fullest part
- Subtract band measurement
- Each inch = one cup size
- AA, A, B, C, D, DD, DDD, G

Gloves
Measure hand circumference at knuckles:
XS: 6-6.5"
S: 6.5-7"
M: 7-7.5"
L: 7.5-8"
XL: 8-8.5"

Hats
Measure head circumference above ears:
XS/S: 21-22"
M/L: 22-23"
XL/XXL: 23-24"
One size: Usually fits 21-24"

Belts
Measure through belt loops:
S: 28-30"
M: 32-34"
L: 36-38"
XL: 40-42"
XXL: 44-46"

Or order 2 sizes up from pant size.
